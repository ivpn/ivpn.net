<p><strong>Please note for this role we are only recruiting within the UTC-1 to UTC+3 timezones.</strong></p>

<p>IVPN is a mission-driven company with a narrow focus on privacy within the larger VPN consumer market. We have been around for 16 years and have a mature, profitable operation. Beyond <a href="https://www.ivpn.net/en/">IVPN</a> we operate <a href="https://www.moddns.net/">modDNS</a> (DNS filtering), <a href="https://www.mailx.net/">Mailx</a> (email aliasing), and <a href="https://safing.io/">Portmaster</a> (application firewall), offered as part of <a href="https://www.ivpn.net/en/pricing/">different IVPN plans</a>.</p>

<p>We are looking for an infrastructure engineer/sysadmin to upgrade and maintain our existing high-performance <a href="https://www.ivpn.net/en/servers/">network of VPN and DNS servers</a> and related infrastructure. The position is fully remote with a high level of autonomy and requires that you thrive in this type of environment.</p>

<h2>What you will work on</h2>

<p>You will work alongside our senior infrastructure engineer / sysadmin, with primary day-to-day responsibility for IVPN and modDNS. You will share knowledge and support each other on shared infrastructure work, but you will own your areas and take on projects independently.</p>

<p>You will:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Roll out our new VPN gateway fleet — immutable, diskless, secure, and built on leased bare-metal servers.</li>
    <li>Deploy new endpoints and engineer routing improvements for our DNS service.</li>
    <li>Ensure the high availability of our network of bare-metal servers (VPN and DNS) in production.</li>
    <li>Drive automation and tooling improvements across our infrastructure.</li>
    <li>Interface with ISPs to resolve upstream network issues.</li>
    <li>Provide tier 2/3 support to the rest of the team for infrastructure-related issues.</li>
    <li>After you settle into your role, share an on-call rotation with the senior on the team.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What you can expect</h2>

<p>We work in a fully remote setting with a high degree of independence. This means maximum freedom, minimum meetings.</p>

<p>If your ideal workplace offers regular meetups and busy chat channels, IVPN is probably not a good fit for you. On the other hand, if you value autonomy and focused work, you will appreciate our culture.</p>

<p>As you grow into the role, there's room to share your work publicly — writing about the infrastructure choices we make, or discussing them with the wider privacy community.</p>

<p>We offer 25 days of vacation per year, the hardware of your choice to do your job, plus ample budget for learning and research projects.</p>

<p>Budget for this role is €48–58K/year (B2B), depending on skills and experience. Mission-driven work, autonomy and flexibility come with the package.</p>

<p>Engagement is on a B2B contract basis, we can't employ across all jurisdictions. In practice this is a long-term role — most of our colleagues have been with us 5+ years.</p>

<h2>How we stand out from the crowd of VPN providers</h2>

<ul>
    <li><a href="https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2018/12/testing-wireguard-with-an-early-adopter-vpn-service/">Early adopters</a> of the latest protocols and technologies (like WireGuard) to improve our service.</li>
    <li>Strict <a href="https://www.ivpn.net/en/ethics/">ethical stances</a> around data collection and marketing practices — principles over profits.</li>
    <li>Clear commitment to <a href="https://github.com/ivpn">transparency</a> and <a href="https://www.ivpn.net/en/blog/tags/audit/">regular security audits</a> (8 years and counting).</li>
    <li>Calling out bad practices in the VPN industry and shunning false promises.</li>
    <li>Recommended <a href="https://www.privacyguides.org/en/vpn/">by experts</a> whose voice we truly value — those not motivated by affiliate payouts.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Requirements</h2>

<ul>
    <li>Strong understanding of and interest in infosec — particularly the cryptographic controls used on servers (public-key crypto, reproducible builds, TPM).</li>
    <li>Strong understanding of TCP/IP, UDP, SSL/TLS, and other Internet protocols.</li>
    <li><strong>3+ years</strong> experience managing Linux servers in production, including bare-metal.</li>
    <li><strong>3+ years</strong> experience with a configuration management tool (Ansible, Puppet, Salt). Demonstrable production codebase or git history preferred.</li>
    <li>Comfortable reading and writing utility scripts in Python, Go, or Bash.</li>
    <li>Automation mindset. You instinctively look for the manual process that should be automated.</li>
    <li>Excellent written communication — you document systems clearly and explain technical decisions so teammates and users can follow them.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Nice to have</h2>

<ul>
    <li>Experience with WireGuard, OpenVPN, or other VPN protocols.</li>
    <li>Experience with anycast network operation (BGP, traffic engineering, RPKI).</li>
    <li>Familiarity with nftables and modern Linux networking internals.</li>
    <li>Curiosity about LLM-assisted operations: using AI agents for log analysis, incident triage, runbook generation. You'll have room to introduce these tools where you see fit.</li>
    <li>You enjoy sharing your work — through writing, talks, or other public formats.</li>
</ul>

<p>Send an email with your CV and any relevant information added to: <a href="mailto:apply@ivpn.net">apply@ivpn.net</a> (subject: Infrastructure Engineer application / IVPN)</p>

    <li>Tell us about a concrete production incident you personally diagnosed — what broke, how you found the cause, what you changed.</li>
</ol>
